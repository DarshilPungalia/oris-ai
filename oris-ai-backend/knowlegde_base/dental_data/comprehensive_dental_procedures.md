# Oris AI Dental Knowledge Base - Comprehensive Procedures Guide

## Preventive Dentistry

### Routine Dental Examination
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes  
**Recommended Frequency:** Every 6 months  
**What's Included:**
- Comprehensive oral health assessment
- Digital radiographs (X-rays) when necessary
- Oral cancer screening
- Gum disease evaluation
- Bite analysis
- Treatment planning consultation
- Oral hygiene education

**Benefits:**
- Early detection of dental issues
- Prevention of costly treatments
- Maintenance of optimal oral health
- Professional cleaning recommendations

### Professional Dental Cleaning (Prophylaxis)
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Recommended Frequency:** Every 6 months  
**Process:**
- Ultrasonic scaling to remove tartar
- Hand scaling for detailed cleaning
- Polishing to remove surface stains
- Fluoride treatment application
- Personalized oral hygiene instruction

**Benefits:**
- Removes plaque and tartar buildup
- Prevents gum disease
- Freshens breath
- Maintains healthy gums
- Reduces risk of cavities

### Deep Cleaning (Scaling and Root Planing)
**Duration:** 1-2 hours per quadrant  
**Indications:** Gum disease (periodontitis)  
**Process:**
- Local anesthesia for comfort
- Deep scaling below gum line
- Root surface smoothing
- Antimicrobial irrigation
- Post-treatment care instructions

**Recovery:**
- Mild discomfort for 24-48 hours
- Avoid hard foods initially
- Gentle oral hygiene
- Follow-up appointment in 4-6 weeks

## Restorative Dentistry

### Composite Fillings (White Fillings)
**Duration:** 30-90 minutes depending on size  
**Material:** Tooth-colored composite resin  
**Advantages:**
- Natural appearance
- Bonds directly to tooth
- Preserves more natural tooth structure
- Mercury-free option
- Immediate functionality

**Procedure:**
1. Local anesthesia application
2. Decay removal and tooth preparation
3. Bonding agent application
4. Composite placement in layers
5. Light curing for hardening
6. Shaping and polishing

**Aftercare:**
- Avoid eating for 2 hours
- Normal function after numbness wears off
- Good oral hygiene maintenance
- Regular dental checkups

### Dental Crowns
**Duration:** 2-3 appointments over 2-3 weeks  
**Types Available:**
- Porcelain (most natural appearance)
- Porcelain-fused-to-metal (strong and aesthetic)
- Gold (most durable)
- Zirconia (strong and natural-looking)

**Indications:**
- Large fillings that compromise tooth structure
- Root canal treated teeth
- Severely worn or broken teeth
- Cosmetic improvements
- Dental implant restorations

**Process:**
1. First visit: Tooth preparation and temporary crown
2. Laboratory fabrication (1-2 weeks)
3. Second visit: Permanent crown placement

### Root Canal Therapy
**Duration:** 1-3 appointments, 60-90 minutes each  
**Purpose:** Save infected or severely damaged teeth  
**Procedure:**
1. Local anesthesia and isolation
2. Access hole creation
3. Pulp and nerve tissue removal
4. Canal cleaning and shaping
5. Disinfection and filling
6. Temporary or permanent restoration

**Success Rate:** 85-97% depending on case complexity  
**Aftercare:**
- Mild discomfort for 2-3 days
- Avoid chewing on treated side initially
- Crown placement recommended for back teeth
- Regular follow-up appointments

## Cosmetic Dentistry

### Teeth Whitening
**Professional In-Office Whitening:**
- Duration: 60-90 minutes
- Results: 3-8 shades lighter
- Uses concentrated whitening gel
- Immediate results
- Custom mouth guard provided for touch-ups

**Take-Home Whitening:**
- Duration: 2-4 weeks of nightly use
- Custom-fitted trays
- Professional-grade whitening gel
- Gradual, natural-looking results
- More comfortable for sensitive teeth

**Maintenance:**
- Avoid staining foods/drinks for 48 hours post-treatment
- Regular touch-up treatments
- Good oral hygiene
- Professional cleanings

### Porcelain Veneers
**Duration:** 2-3 appointments over 2-3 weeks  
**Best For:**
- Chipped or worn teeth
- Discolored teeth resistant to whitening
- Gaps between teeth
- Slightly crooked teeth
- Uneven tooth sizes

**Process:**
1. Consultation and treatment planning
2. Tooth preparation (minimal enamel removal)
3. Temporary veneers placement
4. Laboratory fabrication
5. Final veneer bonding

**Longevity:** 10-20 years with proper care  
**Care Instructions:**
- Avoid biting hard objects
- Don't use teeth as tools
- Wear night guard if grinding
- Regular dental visits

### Dental Bonding
**Duration:** 30-60 minutes per tooth  
**Best For:**
- Small chips or cracks
- Minor gaps
- Discoloration
- Irregular tooth shapes
- Less expensive alternative to veneers

**Procedure:**
1. Tooth surface preparation
2. Bonding agent application
3. Composite resin placement
4. Sculpting and shaping
5. Light curing and polishing

**Maintenance:**
- Avoid staining substances
- Regular oral hygiene
- Professional cleanings
- Touch-ups as needed (5-10 years)

## Oral Surgery

### Tooth Extraction
**Simple Extraction:**
- Duration: 20-40 minutes
- Local anesthesia
- Immediate aftercare instructions
- Healing time: 1-2 weeks

**Surgical Extraction:**
- Duration: 45-90 minutes
- May require sedation
- Sutures placement
- Healing time: 2-4 weeks

**Post-Extraction Care:**
- Bite on gauze for 30-45 minutes
- Avoid rinsing for 24 hours
- Ice application for swelling
- Soft diet for 2-3 days
- Pain medication as prescribed

### Dental Implants
**Duration:** 3-9 months total treatment time  
**Success Rate:** 95-98%  
**Process:**
1. Initial consultation and planning
2. Implant placement surgery
3. Healing period (osseointegration): 3-6 months
4. Abutment placement
5. Crown fabrication and placement

**Benefits:**
- Permanent tooth replacement
- Preserves jawbone
- Natural appearance and function
- No impact on adjacent teeth
- Long-lasting solution (20+ years)

**Candidacy Requirements:**
- Adequate bone density
- Healthy gums
- Good oral hygiene
- Non-smoker (preferred)
- Realistic expectations

## Emergency Dental Care

### Dental Pain Management
**Immediate Steps:**
1. Rinse with warm salt water
2. Apply cold compress externally
3. Take over-the-counter pain relievers
4. Avoid hot/cold foods
5. Contact dental office immediately

**When to Seek Emergency Care:**
- Severe, persistent pain
- Facial swelling
- Fever accompanying dental pain
- Traumatic injury
- Knocked-out tooth
- Lost filling causing pain

### Knocked-Out Tooth (Avulsed Tooth)
**Immediate Action (First 30 minutes critical):**
1. Handle tooth by crown, not root
2. Rinse gently if dirty (don't scrub)
3. Try to reinsert in socket
4. If unable to reinsert, store in milk or saliva
5. Seek emergency dental care immediately

**Prognosis:**
- Best if reimplanted within 30 minutes
- Good success rate up to 2 hours
- Follow-up care essential

### Broken or Chipped Tooth
**Immediate Care:**
1. Save tooth fragments
2. Rinse mouth with warm water
3. Apply cold compress for swelling
4. Cover sharp edges with dental wax
5. Schedule emergency appointment

**Treatment Options:**
- Small chips: Dental bonding
- Large chips: Crown or veneer
- Severe breaks: Root canal and crown
- Non-restorable: Extraction and replacement

## Pediatric Dentistry

### First Dental Visit
**Recommended Age:** By age 1 or within 6 months of first tooth  
**What to Expect:**
- Gentle examination
- Parent/caregiver education
- Fluoride application if appropriate
- Home care instructions
- Establishing dental home

### Preventive Treatments for Children
**Fluoride Treatments:**
- Applied every 6 months
- Strengthens tooth enamel
- Prevents cavities
- Safe and effective

**Dental Sealants:**
- Applied to back teeth
- Protects against cavities
- Typically done age 6-14
- Lasts 5-10 years

### Managing Dental Anxiety in Children
**Techniques:**
- Tell-show-do method
- Positive reinforcement
- Distraction techniques
- Nitrous oxide (laughing gas) if needed
- Creating positive associations

## Gum Disease Treatment

### Gingivitis
**Symptoms:**
- Red, swollen gums
- Bleeding during brushing/flossing
- Bad breath
- Tender gums

**Treatment:**
- Professional cleaning
- Improved oral hygiene
- Antimicrobial rinse
- Regular maintenance

### Periodontitis
**Symptoms:**
- All gingivitis symptoms plus:
- Gum recession
- Loose teeth
- Pus between teeth and gums
- Changes in bite

**Treatment:**
- Scaling and root planing
- Antibiotic therapy
- Surgical intervention if severe
- Ongoing maintenance therapy

**Maintenance:**
- Professional cleanings every 3-4 months
- Excellent home care
- Regular monitoring
- Lifestyle modifications

## Oral Health Tips

### Daily Oral Hygiene
**Essential Steps:**
1. Brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
2. Floss daily
3. Use antimicrobial mouthwash
4. Clean tongue
5. Stay hydrated

**Proper Brushing Technique:**
- Use soft-bristled toothbrush
- 45-degree angle to gums
- Gentle circular motions
- Brush for 2 minutes
- Replace toothbrush every 3 months

### Diet and Oral Health
**Foods to Limit:**
- Sugary snacks and drinks
- Acidic foods and beverages
- Sticky candies
- Hard foods that can crack teeth

**Teeth-Friendly Foods:**
- Dairy products (calcium)
- Leafy greens
- Lean proteins
- Fruits and vegetables
- Water (especially fluoridated)

### Lifestyle Factors
**Habits to Avoid:**
- Smoking and tobacco use
- Excessive alcohol consumption
- Using teeth as tools
- Teeth grinding (seek treatment)
- Poor oral hygiene

**Positive Habits:**
- Regular dental visits
- Consistent oral hygiene
- Healthy diet
- Wearing mouthguards during sports
- Managing stress
